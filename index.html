I am a Postdoctoral Research Associate in the Department of Statistics at the University of Wisconsin-Madison, working with an interdisciplinary research group led by Professor Jun Zhu from the Department of Statistics and Dr. Dan Walsh from the U.S. Geological Survey. My work spans statistical learning, wildlife disease modeling, graph machine learning, and interpretable deep learning.

I earned my PhD in Statistical Science from Washington State University, where I worked with Dr. Daryl DeFord. During my PhD, I collaborated with Los Alamos National Laboratory, completed a summer internship there in 2024, and received the WSU Graduate Teaching Excellence Award in 2023 as a graduate student instructor.

My experience includes teaching lower- and upper-division statistics and mathematics, working as a research assistant with the Analytics and PsychoPharmacology Laboratory (APPL), and providing statistical consulting through the Center for Interdisciplinary Statistical Education and Research (CISER). I also hold a master's degree in Computer Science from WSU and undergraduate and master's degrees in Statistics from Bangladesh.
