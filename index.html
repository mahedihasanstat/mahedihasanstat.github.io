Mahedi Hasan

I completed my PhD in Statistical Science from Washington State University under Dr. Daryl DeFord. My research focuses on developing deep learning models for out-of-distribution (OOD) generalization, designing Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) for node classification and embedding-based latent feature learning. I also worked on change point detection in dynamic networks.

Prior to that, I earned a Master's in Computer Science with a focus on ML and AI from WSU, where I worked with Dr. Yan Yan. I also hold an MPhil in Applied Statistics, a Master's in Statistics, and a Bachelor's in Statistics.

Research Interests

  • Seismic event classification (Earthquakes vs. Explosions)
  • Out-of-distribution (OOD) generalization in deep learning
  • Graph neural networks and latent representation learning
  • Change point detection in dynamic networks

Current Projects

  • Seismic Event Discrimination: Deep learning models trained on synthetic waveforms, tested on real data
  • Change Point Detection: Spectral embeddings + machine learning to identify network changes
  • Fair Network Aggregation: Strategies for fair combination of social subnetworks
